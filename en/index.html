The Mincer earnings function forms the basis for this analysis, focusing on the relationships between education level, work experience, and wages. However, traditional decompositions face challenges due to collinearity between age, birth year, and current year. The HLT approach further dissects wage growth, suggesting that as individuals near retirement, wage growth is primarily influenced by the time effect rather than human capital investment.
